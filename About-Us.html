About The Society

Our Aim

The Johannesburg Amateur Historical Society is an organization dedicated to both research and teaching. We exist to connect members with similar interests and field, especially welcoming all those who are not professional historians but are willing to learn.

The ultimate goal of our society is to build a healthy circle of friends and colleagues who can contribute to spreading knowledge about our amazing city and it's history. We aim to hold regular public events as well as lectures and other opportunities such as community engagements and school activities.

All are welcome at JAHS and we look forward to hearing more stories and perspectives from this city full of history!

The Current Board

Simon Wessels

A resident of Bez Valley and a native Joburger, Simon is the resident expert on the tumultuous history of the Rand Rebellion as well as Joburg's many historically significant buildings.

In lieu of certain members not being able to take up their duties, Simon is the main person responsbile for the hosting of events as well as the societie's treasurer. He can found as a regular at the Woodpecker Bowls Club in Bez Valley, hosting many meetups and talks for the society there.
